Team Profile

Welcome to the Drive Aware Tech Team (DAT Team) group page. Drive Aware is our teams proposed project that will be developed in-line with RMIT's Introduction to Information Technology assessment work. Our aim is to develop Drive Aware from theoretically stage to tangible results towards a working prototype. Drive Aware is an App based project which will work in conjunction with wearable technology to track and monitor a users biometric vital signs and fatigue indicators associated with long periods of sustained driving.

The specialist roles I engage with range from Developers, Analysts, Architects, Network specialists, etc. I love to learn about the different aspects from our business perspective because it amazes me what can be achieved.

My strengths would have to be my Communication skills, Organisational skills and my enthusiasm for learning. I believe with effort you can figure out most things.

Gerard Samson-Dekker – Student: S3882545




BACKGROUND


Originally from Sydney, I’ve been lucky to benefit from quite a varied upbringing, being schooled in several countries before living in Scotland for most of my adult life. I studied pharmacy after leaving school, graduating in 2005 before completing registration training, and passing the examination for registration. After this I decided to work freelance as a locum in order to gain as much varied experience of the sector as I could. I was responsible for finding work, building a good reputation as well as managing my own schedule and accounts. I really enjoyed the nature of this role, needing to adjust to new work environments and team members quickly in order to do my job while maintaining accountability and responsibility for my own work. Because I enjoyed it so much, after telling myself that I would do it for a year or so, I ended up continuing the role one and off for over ten years. During this time I met my wife and we went on to have two children.

A few years after qualifying, personal events meant a change of scene and some spare time so I decided to study laws of international business, an area that interested me and that I thought a knowledge of would prove useful in future. I thoroughly enjoyed this, especially competition and EU law, writing my master’s thesis on EU competition law, parallel trade and its impact on the pharmaceutical industry. I had not intended that the degree would lead to any specific career change and although it has lead to some career opportunities. I see its real value in having improved my evaluation of problems and resultant decision making meaning that what once would may have seemed overwhelming now appears achievable. I had considered myself to be a critical thinker as I had completed a scientific degree but had now learnt another way to assess situations, identify relevant issues and solve problems.

Recently we accepted opportunity for our family to move back to Australia. Registering as a pharmacist in Australia having UK qualifications is surprisingly difficult and there isn’t perhaps the demand for my skills as there was in Scotland. Rather than spend time and money on this, I saw it as an opportunity give my career a steer in another direction. I have been involved in a few projects related to IT which I had found very engaging and had always wondered about being able to improve on the systems with which I have worked. Having studied online before, I was confident I would have the self discipline to repeat this and knew that although it presents challenges, I value both its flexibility and portability.
